As people around the world continue to physically distance themselves, more than ever they are coming together and sharing what they can. We are not alone and we are not isolated. The kindness contagion is evident in communities around the world. Just some of the stories below are examples of the great humanity that people are showing in their communities.

InVaLUaBLE

Assistant head teacher, Zane Powles, in England walks 5 miles every day delivering meals to his students who rely on school for lunch. He says “the discomfort of carrying heavy food around the empty streets in his rucksack is nothing compared with the ordeal faced by families forced to stay at home.”

NEIGHBORLY

It is so important now, to reach out to our neighbors and check in on them. In Batavia, IL neighborhoods are organizing fun walks! This entails people hanging up the towns mascot in their window and chalking the sidewalks to give families with kids something to look at and interact with as they get fresh air around the neighborhood.

THAnKFUL

In New York City, as nurses do their shift changes at 8PM every night, New Yorkers open their windows, clap, yell, and flash their lights in celebration of the hard work that hospital workers put in day in and day out to save lives.

SUPPORT

Rollins Distillery has converted their distillery from producing beer into producing hand sanitizer. This change of production gives the local community a chance to support their favorite local business, but also provides others with a very sought after product that helps to fight against COVID-19.

GEnEROSITY

Grocery stores such as Aldi, Target, Walmart, and Costco have started holding “senior hour” in the morning before their stores are opened to the public. This allows the elderly, people with underlying health issues, and those with physical impairments to have time to shop before the stores become crowded with people. Allowing them to take what they need without the worry that it might go out of stock and be contaminated.
